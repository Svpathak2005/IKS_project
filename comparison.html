Hindu Temple Architecture

Somnath Temple
Nagara Style / North Indian Style

Key Features of Nagara Architecture

Shikhara: Curvilinear spire with rounded contours
Malaka: Circular crowning element at the top
Mandapas: Multiple peaked halls creating dynamic silhouette
Overall Style: Vertical emphasis with graceful curves
Brihadeshwara Temple
Dravidian Style / South Indian Style

Key Features of Dravidian Architecture

Vimana: Pyramidal tower with straight edges
Capstone: Square element at the summit
Structure: Successively smaller tiers
Overall Style: Horizontal emphasis with monumental grandeur

Shared Elements

Despite their distinct visual languages, both styles incorporate:

  • The axis mundi (cosmic axis) connecting earth to heaven
  • The garbhagriha (sanctum sanctorum) housing the deity
  • The plinth (base platform) elevating the structure
  • Mandapas (halls) for worshippers

This architectural divergence reflects regional cultural expressions of a shared spiritual tradition, demonstrating how ancient Indian architects adapted temple designs to local aesthetic sensibilities and construction materials.
